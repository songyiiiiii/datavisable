Cosmic Density Evolution — Statistical Analysis

Key findings: Over 100 time steps, the density standard deviation σ increased by +15.4% (from 0.432 to 0.498), the IQR grew by +16.7%, and the P99−P01 spread widened by +15.1%. The minimum density dropped from 7.984 to 7.753 (voids deepen), while the maximum rose from 13.843 to 14.449 (peaks intensify). These trends collectively quantify the irreversible clumpification driven by dark matter gravitational collapse — the density field transitions from a narrow, symmetric distribution toward a broad, positively-skewed, bipolarized state.

Density Log-Histogram Temporal Tracking

Key findings: Using absolute density thresholds (ln(rho) < 8.5 for voids, ln(rho) > 12.0 for peaks), the void volume fraction grew from 0.31% to 2.99% (+2.68 pp), while the distribution mode shifted from 9.404 to 9.229 (-0.176). The FWHM increased by +14.3% (0.820 to 0.938). The temporal derivative heatmap reveals mass transfer from intermediate densities toward both tails — the hallmark of gravitational bipolarization.
